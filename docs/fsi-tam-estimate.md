# FSI TAM Estimate For Exa

## Definition

This estimates Exa-capturable ARR for financial-services agent retrieval, not total financial-services software spend.

Two customer types:

1. Direct FSI institutions: banks, asset managers, brokerages, insurers, payments companies, lenders, and wealth platforms with internal AI/platform teams.
2. Platform/vendor buyers: core banking, digital banking, lending, wealth, advisor-tech, and compliance platforms that embed Exa into workflows used by smaller FSI customers.

Smaller FSI firms are counted as downstream end users, not usually the best direct Exa enterprise customers.

## How To Say It In The Take-Home

The credible wording:

> On a logo basis, the direct enterprise opportunity is roughly 1,500-3,500 global financial institutions, with 500-1,500 high-priority early logos. A second platform-vendor wedge adds roughly 150-500 high-fit infrastructure and workflow vendors that distribute into tens of thousands of smaller financial institutions. On a revenue basis, this is plausibly a several-hundred-million-dollar ARR opportunity for Exa, with a path toward $1B+ if Exa becomes the external-intelligence layer embedded across FSI agent platforms.

Do not frame the TAM as all banks, all RIAs, and all insurers buying Exa directly. That overstates the direct motion. The sharper claim is:

> Enterprise FSI buys Exa directly; smaller FSI expands the market through platform distribution and narrow PLG applets.

## Recommended TAM Narrative

Use three layers:

1. Direct enterprise TAM: where Exa sells to AI/platform teams building internal agents.
2. Platform TAM: where Exa sells to vendors embedding retrieval into banking, lending, wealth, compliance, and advisor workflows.
3. Downstream end-user TAM: the broad smaller-FSI market that validates demand but is not necessarily the first sales motion.
